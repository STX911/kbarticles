Chest Pain
Useful Information

Common Signs and Symptoms of a Heart Attack may include:


Pain is most often felt in the center of the chest and may spread to the shoulder, arm, or jaw, or may be felt only in these areas. The pain of heart attack is usually described as a ‘pressure’, ‘heaviness’, or ‘tightness’. Often associated with:
  • Difficulty in breathing
  • Profuse sweating
  • Nausea and/or vomiting
  • Cool, pale, moist skin; possible bluish in color
  • Weakness
  • Anxiety
Critical Problems:
  • Heart attack (Myocardial Infarction or MI)
  • Ruptured thoracic aortic aneurysm
  • Tension pneumothorax
Potentially Critical problems:
  • Pulmonary embolus (blood clot in lungs)
  • Pneumonia in the very young and old
  • Pericarditis
  • Pneumothorax
  • Ulcers
Non-Critical Problems:
  • Pleurisy
  • Pneumonia (if very old or young, then potentially critical)
  • Esophagitis, reflux
  • Hiatal hernia
  • Viral illness
  • Anxiety

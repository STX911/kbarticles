Head and Neck Injuries
Useful Information

A victim with a serious and unstable spinal fracture may be up and ambulatory. However, the slightest wrong movement could suddenly impact the patient's spinal cord. If the mechanism of injury is suspicious, then THE SPINE MUST BE PROTECTED.

With the exception of maintaining the Airway, Breathing and Circulation, the single most important thing to be done for the possible spine injury patient is to prevent further movement and injury.

The only valid reason for moving a patient with suspected head/spinal injury is to protect from further injury or deterioration. If a patient must be moved for some reason, the body must be moved as a unit, keeping "the nose in line with the belly button" and without flexing the head and neck.

Bystanders will often want to move the patient. Be firm and specific when instructing the caller not to move the patient.

Nausea, vomiting, dizziness and altered mental status common in severe head injury are also common in simple concussions. BE ALERT for vomiting, it is very common in head injury.

Cervical Spine Fractures, can paralyze the muscles of breathing.

Modified Jaw Thrust technique will open airway without moving spine.

Childbirth
Useful Information

The vast majority of births, however painful and emotionally stressful, are without complication. Keep talking to the mother and be encouraging through the entire process. Note: Most infants require no resuscitation other than warmth, mild stimulation and keeping the upper airway clear.

Stages of Pregnancy

  • 1st Trimester = 0 to 3 months
  • 2nd Trimester = 4 to 6 months
  • 3rd Trimester = 7 to 9 months

Note:In general, patients in their initial pregnancy progress through labor more slowly than subsequent pregnancies.

Signs of Imminent Birth

Includes contractions less than 3 minutes apart, feeling the need to have a bowel movement, "pushing" with the contractions or seeing part of the baby.
[Return]


Abnormal (Breech) Presentations Include:

  • Buttocks deliver first
  • Arm or leg deliver first
  • Prolapsed umbilical cord

Note: Limb presentation and prolapsed cord are life threatening emergencies. Rapid Transport to a hospital is critical.
[Return]


Supine Hypotensive Syndrome

At times, when the mother lies on her back, the uterus pressing on the blood vessels, prevents blood from returning to the heart. Turning her on her side will relieve the pressure and restore circulation.

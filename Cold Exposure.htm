Cold Exposure
Useful Information

Frostbitten areas will be numb, but may become painful and tingling as they thaw. They should never be placed directly against an open flame or hot dry heat source, like a car tail pipe.

·         Tissue damage is significantly increased if a thawed part is allowed to refreeze. Therefore, never begin actively re-warming frost bitten parts unless you can make sure it will stay warm. Re-warming is usually accomplished by bathing the part In warm water, but the temperature must be very precise and this would be impossible to determine in the field.

Hypothermia: As body temperature lowers, there is a progressive depression of mental, motor and cardio respiratory function.

·         Remember: It doesn't have to be freezing to cause hypothermia. Wind chill, water exposure, clothing, patient's age and medical condition all playa role.

·         Hypothermic patients may be mistaken to be intoxicated.

Substances that interfere with the body's normal response to cold (drugs, alcohol) and medical conditions affecting circulation (i.e., diabetes, heart disease) make one more susceptible.

The thermo regulatory system (body temperature control) in the very young and very old does not function as well and makes them more susceptible to cold, as well as heat illness.

Pediatric considerations: Young children are seldom aware of early signs of cold exposure, are less apt to seek shelter, and wear appropriate clothing. Also, the very young are dependent on responsible caretakers to provide adequate protection and preventive measures for them.

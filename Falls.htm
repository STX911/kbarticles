TR Knowledgebase: Falls

Falls -- Useful Information

Long fall, over 15 feet, onto an unyielding surface is considered severe. Internal organs often rupture or are tom from their attachments, resulting in internal hemorrhage and/ or shock.

Consider that the patient's fall may be the result of a medical problem (fainting, heart attack, seizure, stroke, arrhythmia, etc.) or electrocution.

Hip fractures are common in the elderly.

Falls are the most common mechanism of injury to cause trauma.
Severity depends on:

  • Height of fall
  • Anything that interrupts the fall
  • Landing surface
  • Which body part impacts

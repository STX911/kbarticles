TR Knowledgebase: Headaches

Headaches
Useful Information

Non-Traumatic Types/Causes:

  • Tension
  • Cluster
  • Sinus
  • Migraine
  • Meningitis
  • Subarachnoid Hemorrhage - Due to a ruptured intracranial aneurysm. Frequently called "the worst headache I ever had". It is often preceded days before by a severe headache, which resolved spontaneously and goes undiagnosed. (This is called a Sentinel Headache)
Traumatic Types/Causes:
  • Subdural Hematoma - May develop slowly over weeks or months following an injury. Especially common in alcoholics and elderly.

Alterations in mental status, speech and motor or sensory functions may reflect more serious conditions, with headache as the initial complaint.

Sudden, severe onset may suggest a more serious underlying cause.

The dispatcher should be alert to possible environmental hazards (carbon monoxide, natural gas, etc.).

The vast majority of headaches are caused by structures surrounding the brain and the skull, and not the brain itself.

A migraine is a "great pretender." It may be associated with temporary paralysis or loss of vision, mimicking other more serious conditions.

Neck stiffness is common in subarachnoid hemorrhage and meningitis.

Post Concussive Syndrome - Headache and dizziness may persist for weeks and even months following a simple concussion.

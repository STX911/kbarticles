Preparing and Protecting Law Enforcement Responders in Active Shooting Incidents

 

Guidelines

•         Obtain specific intelligence as it pertains to the exact location of the Active Shooter

•         Gain sufficient information from caller by applying the Journalistic-Investigative Approach: 5W + H + W + H + I

•         Use "Inverted Visualization" when questioning caller and gain specific information from officer's perspective as he/she arrives

•         Become more "Victim Focused" and obtain information also related to the caller/victim: Where are they specifically? Do they have any weapons?

•         Keep responders updated and eliminate surprises

•         Continue to inform regarding location of reported sounds of gun shots

•         Inform responders when additional descriptions are received from additional callers

•         Remember each new caller is potentially a new witness, with new and changing information

•         Think in terms of potential multiples

•         Shooters, victims, responders, agencies

•         Multiples will always complicate the response

•         Gather information regarding all possible areas of egress and entrance to target building

•         Including additional elevators  (passenger or commercial)

•         Stair wells

•         Areas of egress maybe locked by suspect

•         Initiate integrated communication under your Incident Command System:

•         Effective two-way communications is essential to effective incident management

•         Managing communications at an incident through use of a common communications plan

•         Established procedures for common terminology and clear text
